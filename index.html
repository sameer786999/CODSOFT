Pricing

In a market economy, prices are typically determined by the forces of supply and demand. When demand for a product or service exceeds its supply, prices tend to rise, and vice versa. Various factors can influence pricing decisions, including production costs, competition, consumer preferences, and external economic conditions.Pricing strategies vary across industries and businesses. Companies may adopt different approaches, such as cost-based pricing, where prices are set based on production and operational expenses, or value-based pricing, which considers the perceived value of a product or service to customers. Dynamic pricing, where prices change based on real-time demand and supply conditions, is also common, especially in online markets.
